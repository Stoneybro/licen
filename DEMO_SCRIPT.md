# LICEN Demo Script

## Goal

This is a **product-first demo** for judges. The goal is not to explain the engineering stack in depth. The goal is to make judges immediately understand:

1. What problem LICEN solves
2. Who it is for
3. How the product flow works
4. Why the approach is defensible
5. Why this can become a real business

## Core Positioning

Use this one-line framing early:

> LICEN is a marketplace where dataset owners can safely monetize AI training access without giving away the raw data.

And this sharper version if you want a stronger line:

> We turned a data license from a PDF into an enforceable product workflow.

## Recommended Demo Length

- **Best version:** 4 minutes
- **Short version:** 3 minutes
- **Expanded version with questions baked in:** 5 minutes

## Suggested Demo Structure

1. Problem
2. Product
3. Live flow
4. Why now / why this wins
5. Business potential

---

## Main Script

## 1. Opening Hook

“AI companies need specialized data, but access to high-quality private datasets is still broken.

If you are a lab, institution, or independent data creator, you usually have only two bad options: either keep your dataset locked away and earn nothing, or share it and lose control over it.

LICEN fixes that. We let dataset owners monetize model training access while keeping the raw dataset protected and the usage policy in their hands.”

Pause.

“So this is not just a storage product, and it is not just a licensing tool. It is a product that turns controlled dataset access into a transaction that can actually happen.”

## 2. Who This Is For

“LICEN is built for two sides of the market.

On one side are dataset owners: research labs, biomedical institutions, legal data holders, and independent curators with valuable niche data.

On the other side are AI teams and researchers who urgently need high-quality domain-specific data, but do not want months of legal back-and-forth or risky data handling.

We connect both sides in one workflow.”

## 3. Product Walkthrough

“Let me show the product from both perspectives.”

### Dataset Owner Flow

“First, the dataset owner uploads a dataset and defines the usage policy.

They set things like the price, the permitted use, how much training can happen in one run, how often the same buyer can return, and how long access stays valid.

The important product promise here is simple: they stay in control. They are not dumping a file on the internet and hoping for fair use later. They are publishing access on their own terms, inside an enforceable policy boundary.”

### Researcher Flow

“Now from the researcher side, they browse the marketplace, discover a relevant dataset, understand the policy immediately, and pay for the exact training access they need.

From their perspective, LICEN removes the usual friction:

- no long negotiation cycle
- no unclear licensing terms
- no custom data infrastructure to build

They get a clean path from discovery to payment to trained output.”

## 4. The Moment That Matters

“This is the core product insight:

The buyer does not need the raw dataset. What they actually want is the model improvement that comes from training on that dataset.

LICEN is designed around that reality.

Instead of selling the file itself, we enable secure paid training access inside owner-defined policy constraints. That means the dataset owner can capture value without surrendering the asset.”

## 5. What Happens After Payment

“Once the researcher pays, the workflow is triggered automatically.

The training job runs, the owner's usage policy is enforced through the product flow, and when the training completes, the dataset owner receives royalties automatically.

So the outcome is:

- the creator gets paid
- the researcher gets the trained result
- the raw dataset remains protected

That is the user experience LICEN is delivering.”

## 6. Why This Is Better Than Existing Alternatives

“Today, most data licensing still depends on contracts, email threads, manual trust, and closed negotiations.

That does not scale for the future of AI.

LICEN is better because it is:

- faster for researchers
- safer for dataset owners
- clearer commercially
- more repeatable as a marketplace product

We are not asking people to trust a promise. We are giving them a workflow they can use.”

## 7. Builder Angle

“What excites me most is that LICEN creates a new primitive for the AI economy.

We are giving builders a way to work with premium data that was previously inaccessible, while giving data owners a credible reason to participate instead of staying on the sidelines.

If better models require better data, then the infrastructure for controlled data monetization becomes extremely valuable.

That is the category we are building into.”

## 8. Business Angle

“This is also a real business, not just a demo.

We make money through a protocol fee on successful royalty settlements, so the model scales with marketplace usage.

That aligns us with both sides:

- creators only win when they earn
- researchers only pay when they get access
- LICEN only grows when transactions happen

Longer term, this can also expand into enterprise deployments for organizations with highly sensitive internal data.”

## 9. Close

“So the simplest way to think about LICEN is this:

we help people monetize valuable datasets for AI training without giving the data away.

That makes proprietary data more usable, AI training more accessible, and dataset ownership economically meaningful.

That is LICEN.”

## Screen-by-Screen Demo Flow

Use this if you are clicking through the actual product.

1. **Start on the landing page or docs**
   Say what LICEN is in one sentence.

2. **Show the publisher flow**
   Point out upload, policy-setting, and control over access rules.

3. **Show the marketplace / researcher view**
   Point out discoverability, clear terms, and fast path to access.

4. **Show the payment / access trigger moment**
   Explain that payment initiates the controlled training workflow.

5. **Show the dashboard or status tracking**
   Point out royalties, active sessions, or job progress.

6. **End with business model / why this matters**
   Return to the value: protected data, faster access, automatic monetization.

## What Not To Do

- Do not start with smart contracts, TEE, encryption, or chain architecture
- Do not spend more than 20 to 30 seconds on technical implementation
- Do not narrate button clicks without tying them to user value
- Do not assume judges already understand why dataset access is a problem
- Do not make it sound like a research prototype; present it like a product category

## How To Talk About the Tech Without Turning It Into an Engineering Demo

Use one short line only:

> Under the hood, LICEN uses secure infrastructure and automated on-chain settlement so access control and royalties are enforced by the system, not by paperwork.

That is enough unless a judge asks a technical follow-up.

## Visual Aids You Should Use

Yes, you should use visual aids. For judges, they will help a lot.

### 1. One opening slide

You should have **one title slide** with:

- LICEN name
- one-line value proposition
- one-line audience framing

Suggested subtitle:

> Monetize AI training access without exposing the raw dataset

### 2. One simple flow diagram

You should absolutely have **one clean product flow diagram**.

Keep it product-level, not infrastructure-level:

`Dataset Owner uploads -> Sets usage policy -> Researcher discovers dataset -> Pays for training access -> Model is trained -> Owner gets royalties`

This is the single most useful visual aid for judges.

### 3. One business slide

Use **one slide** that shows:

- creator side
- researcher side
- LICEN in the middle
- protocol fee / royalty flow

This helps judges see that it is a marketplace, not just a tool.

### 4. Optional backup slide

Have one backup slide for Q&A:

- why this matters now
- why it is defensible
- how you make money

Do not present this unless needed.

## Minimum Visual Package

If time is tight, prepare only these:

1. Title slide
2. Product flow diagram
3. Live product demo

That is enough.

## Strong Judge-Friendly Phrases

Use these lines naturally during the demo:

- “We are not selling the dataset file; we are selling controlled training access.”
- “Purpose is only one field. The real product is enforceable usage policy.”
- “The raw data stays protected, but the value of the data becomes monetizable.”
- “For researchers, this compresses legal and operational friction into a simple product flow.”
- “For creators, this turns passive data ownership into recurring revenue.”
- “This is a marketplace product with a credible revenue model, not just an infrastructure demo.”

## Likely Judge Questions

### Why would dataset owners trust this?

“Because the product is designed so they keep control over access terms and get paid automatically when usage happens.”

### Why would researchers use this instead of existing channels?

“Because it is faster, clearer, and better suited to niche data where access is currently fragmented and slow.”

### Is this a real business or just a hackathon prototype?

“It has a direct transaction-based revenue model, a clear two-sided market, and an obvious expansion path into enterprise-sensitive data workflows.”

### What is the wedge?

“The wedge is specialized, high-value datasets where the demand is real but access is difficult and current licensing workflows are too slow.”

## Final Advice

In the demo, your job is not to prove that the system is technically sophisticated. The repo already proves you built something serious.

Your job is to make judges believe three things:

1. This problem is real
2. This product solves it cleanly
3. This can become a meaningful company
